## 1. Introduction

These two units cover the fundamental English tenses: the Present Continuous and the Present Simple. They are essential for distinguishing between things happening right now and things that happen generally. In this summary, we will cover Unit 1 and Unit 2 to build a strong foundation for present-tense English.

---

## 2. Unit 1: Present Continuous

### Grammar Rule

We use the Present Continuous to talk about actions or situations that are happening **at the time of speaking** or **around the present time**. 

* The action is ongoing and not yet complete.
* We form it by using the present tense of the verb **to be** (am/is/are) followed by the **-ing** form of the main verb.

### Form Table

| Subject | Positive | Negative | Question |
| - | - | - | - |
| I | am working (*I'm*) | am not working (*I'm not*) | Am I working? |
| He / She / It | is working (*he's*) | is not working (*isn't*) | Is she working? |
| We / You / They | are working (*they're*) | are not working (*aren't*) | Are you working? |

### Key Examples

**Positive**
* Please be quiet. I **am working**.
* Look at Sue! She **is wearing** her new hat.
* The weather is nice. It **is not raining**.

**Negative**
* We **are not watching** TV right now.
* He **is not doing** his homework.
* They **aren't playing** football.

**Question**
* **Are** you **listening** to me?
* Why **are** they **laughing**?
* What **is** she **eating**?

### When to Use

| Situation | Example |
| - | - |
| Actions happening right now | 'Where's Mark?' 'He **is having** a shower.' |
| Temporary situations happening around now | I **am reading** a really good book at the moment. |
| Changing or developing situations | The population of the world **is increasing** very fast. |

### Common Mistakes

```text
❌ I work at the moment, so I will call you later.
✅ I am working at the moment, so I will call you later.

❌ Listen to those people. What language do they speak?
✅ Listen to those people. What language are they speaking?
```

## 3. Unit 2: Present Simple

### Grammar Rule

We use the Present Simple to talk about things in general. It describes facts that are always true, repeated actions or habits, and situations that are permanent.

* The main verb takes the base form for most subjects.
* For **he, she, and it**, the verb takes an **-s** or **-es** ending. 
* For negative and question forms, we use the auxiliary verb **do** or **does**.

### Form Table

| Subject | Positive | Negative | Question |
| - | - | - | - |
| I / We / You / They | work | do not work (*don't*) | Do you work? |
| He / She / It | works | does not work (*doesn't*) | Does he work? |

### Key Examples

**Positive**
* Nurses **look** after patients in hospitals.
* The earth **goes** round the sun.
* He **always arrives** early for meetings.

**Negative**
* I **don't go** to the cinema very often.
* She **doesn't drink** tea.
* Rice **doesn't grow** in cold climates.

**Question**
* Excuse me, **do** you **speak** English?
* What **does** this word **mean**?
* **Do** they **play** tennis every weekend?

### When to Use

| Situation | Example |
| - | - |
| General facts and universal truths | Water **boils** at 100 degrees Celsius. |
| Habits and repeated actions | I usually **leave** for work at 8 AM. |
| Permanent situations | My parents **live** in London. |

### Common Mistakes

```text
❌ He work in a bank.
✅ He works in a bank.

❌ What does he does?
✅ What does he do?
```

## 📝 Quick Summary

**Unit 1:**
- Use the Present Continuous to describe actions happening right now or around now.
- Don't forget: The action is temporary and not complete yet.

**Unit 2:**
- Use the Present Simple to describe habits, facts, and permanent situations.
- Don't forget: Add -s or -es for third-person singular subjects (he, she, it) in positive sentences.
